CSI Section 08 42 29 — Automatic Sliding Door Systems

SECTION 08 42 29

AUTOMATIC SLIDING DOOR SYSTEMS
Manufacturer: Doortronix Inc., Malakoff, TX Model: DT-3000 Revolution Reference Standard: ANSI/BHMA A156.10
Specifier Note This specification is written in CSI 3-part SectionFormat for the Doortronix DT-3000 Revolution automatic sliding door system. Part 1 — General

1.01 Summary

A. Section includes power-operated, microprocessor-controlled automatic sliding door systems, including operators, door panels, sidelights, headers, hardware, sensors, controls, and accessories.

B. Related Requirements:

1. Section 08 11 13 — Hollow Metal Doors and Frames.

2. Section 08 80 00 — Glazing.

3. Section 26 05 00 — Common Work Results for Electrical, for power supply to door operators.

Specifier Note Edit the related sections above to reference the actual section numbers used in the project manual. Delete any that do not apply.

1.02 References

A. American National Standards Institute / Builders Hardware Manufacturers Association (ANSI/BHMA):

1. ANSI/BHMA A156.10 — Power Operated Pedestrian Doors.

B. International Code Council (ICC):

1. ICC A117.1 — Accessible and Usable Buildings and Facilities.

C. National Fire Protection Association (NFPA):

1. NFPA 101 — Life Safety Code, for means of egress.

D. Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) Standards for Accessible Design.

E. Canadian Standards Association (CSA): CSA B651 — Accessible Design for the Built Environment.

F. National Electrical Code (NFPA 70 / NEC).

1.03 Submittals

A. Product Data: Manufacturer's data sheets for each system, component, and accessory specified, including operator, controller, sensors, finishes, and electrical requirements.

B. Shop Drawings: Show elevations, configurations, panel arrangement, rough opening dimensions, header detail, anchorage, glazing, breakout direction, sensor locations, electrical rough-in, and clearances.

C. Samples: Finish samples for each specified anodized or coated finish, upon request.

D. Quality Assurance Submittals:

1. Manufacturer's certification of compliance with ANSI/BHMA A156.10.

2. Installer qualifications, where required.

E. Operation and Maintenance Data: Include in project closeout documents.

F. Warranty: Submit manufacturer's standard and extended warranty documents.

1.04 Quality Assurance

A. Manufacturer Qualifications: Single manufacturer regularly engaged in the production of automatic sliding door systems, with electronics manufactured in the United States.

B. Installer Qualifications: Installation performed by personnel trained in accordance with manufacturer's requirements. Inspection of completed installation by an AAADM-certified inspector is recommended where required by the AHJ.

C. Regulatory Requirements: Comply with ANSI/BHMA A156.10 and applicable accessibility and life-safety codes for the project location.

1.05 Delivery, Storage, and Handling

A. Deliver materials in manufacturer's original, undamaged packaging with identifying labels intact.

B. Store materials indoors, protected from weather, moisture, and construction traffic, in accordance with manufacturer's instructions.

C. Handle materials to prevent damage to finished surfaces and operating components.

1.06 Project Conditions

A. Field Measurements: Verify rough opening dimensions and conditions at the project site before fabrication. Coordinate with related work.

B. Do not install operators until building enclosure is weather-tight and ambient conditions are within manufacturer's limits.

1.07 Warranty

A. Manufacturer's Standard Warranty: One (1) year limited warranty against defects in materials and workmanship for the complete door system, from date of [Substantial Completion] [shipment].

B. Extended Warranty: Five (5) year warranty on the steel drive rod and linear motion bearing assembly.

Specifier Note Confirm warranty commencement date and any project-specific extended warranty terms with the manufacturer prior to issuing for construction.

B. Basis-of-Design Product: DT-3000 "Revolution" Automatic Sliding Door System.

C. Substitutions: [No substitutions permitted.] [Comparable products complying with this Section will be considered in accordance with Section 01 25 00 — Substitution Procedures.]

2.02 Automatic Door Operator

A. Model: Doortronix DT-3000.

B. Drive System: One-inch (1") chrome steel rod with precision linear motion bearing. Track-and-roller drive systems are not permitted.

C. Motor: Direct current (DC), 1/5 HP, variable speed.

D. Drive Belt: Kevlar-reinforced timing belt.

E. Controller: Microprocessor-based control with integral LED self-diagnostic readout displaying fault codes. Servicing shall not require a proprietary handheld programmer.

F. Cycle Rating: Minimum 10,000,000 cycles.

G. Opening Speed: Field-adjustable, from standard commercial through high-speed operation, within the limits of ANSI/BHMA A156.10.

H. Power Supply: 120V AC standard; [208V] [240V] available where indicated.

I. Battery Backup: [Provide] [Do not provide] battery backup to maintain operation during loss of primary power.

2.03 Header

A. Header: Structural aluminum I-beam designed for zero deflection under operating load across the specified clear opening. Extruded channel headers subject to deflection are not permitted.

B. Header shall fully house the operator, drive, and controller, with access for service.

2.04 Door Panels, Sidelights, and Framing

A. Panel Assembly: Extruded aluminum stiles and rails, clip-and-screw mechanically assembled. Welded or bonded panel frames are not permitted. Glass shall be replaceable without panel destruction or special tools.

B. Interlocks: Full-height extruded aluminum interlocks at meeting stiles, top to bottom, with no air-infiltration or light gap.

C. Sidelights: Magnetic breakaway sidelights providing full-width release with consistent breakout force, resettable by hand without tools. Ball-catch retention is not permitted.

D. Bottom Guide: Fixed pin guide with no moving parts. Floor-mounted roller or wheel guides are not permitted.

2.05 Configurations

A. Configuration: As indicated on Drawings. Available configurations include:

1. Bi-parting (standard arrangement SO-SX-SX-SO).

2. Single slide, left-hand (LH) or right-hand (RH) as indicated.

B. Breakout Type: [FBO — Full Breakout] [FSL — Full Sidelight breakout] [Fixed sidelight / non-breakout] as indicated and as required by the AHJ for egress.

Specifier Note Indicate configuration, handing, and breakout type on the Drawings. Many jurisdictions require breakout (FBO/FSL) for emergency egress in high-occupancy applications — verify with the AHJ.

2.06 Finishes

A. Aluminum Finish: [204 Clear Anodized] [313 Dark Bronze Anodized] [Black] [White] [Custom powder coat to match _____].

B. Finish shall be applied uniformly and free of visible defects.

2.07 Glazing

A. Glazing furnished and installed under Section 08 80 00 — Glazing, unless otherwise indicated. Door framing shall be factory-prepared (prepped) for the specified glass thickness.

B. Glass Type: [Clear tempered] [Low-E] [Tinted] [Frosted] [Lead-lined], thickness [1/4"] [9/16"] [5/8"] [1"] as indicated.

Specifier Note Doortronix supplies the aluminum door package only. Glass/glazing is supplied and installed by others. Coordinate glass type and thickness with the Glazing Section and confirm framing glass-prep with the manufacturer.

2.08 Activation and Safety Sensors

A. Activation: [Microwave motion] [Active infrared] [Push-plate / actuator] sensors as indicated.

B. Safety: Door-mounted presence-detection sensors complying with ANSI/BHMA A156.10 to protect the path of door travel.

2.09 Accessibility and Operation

A. When configured to accessible (ADA) timing and opening-force parameters, system shall comply with the ADA Standards and ICC A117.1.

2.10 Fabrication

A. Fabricate units to sizes shown, with components factory-assembled to the greatest extent practicable.

B. Electronics shall be manufactured in the United States.

Part 3 — Execution

3.01 Examination

A. Verify that rough openings, structural support, and electrical rough-in are complete, correctly located, and within tolerances before installation.

B. Do not proceed with installation until unsatisfactory conditions have been corrected.

3.02 Installation

A. Install systems plumb, level, and true to line in accordance with manufacturer's approved shop drawings and printed installation instructions.

B. Anchor securely to adjacent construction. Maintain clearances required for proper operation and breakout.

C. Connect to building electrical supply under Division 26. Coordinate final connections.

3.03 Field Quality Control

A. Adjust operators for proper opening/closing speed, check-out, hold-open time, and breakout force in accordance with ANSI/BHMA A156.10.

B. Where required by the AHJ, arrange inspection by an AAADM-certified inspector and affix the daily safety-check label.

3.04 Adjusting and Cleaning

A. Adjust operating components for smooth, quiet operation.

B. Clean exposed surfaces and glazing; remove labels and protective coverings.

3.05 Demonstration

A. Demonstrate proper operation, safety features, and routine maintenance to the Owner's personnel.

B. Provide the LED fault-code reference and maintenance documentation.
