Revolution Sliding Door — DT-3000

Rod-and-bearing drive eliminates track entirely. I-beam header. Self-diagnosing controller. No programmer required. 1-year warranty + 5-year on rod & bearing.

A Fundamentally Different Drive System

Every other automatic sliding door on the market uses a track-and-roller system. When rollers wear, panels drop. When debris collects in the track, the door binds. "Door off track" accounts for 85% of all automatic door service calls.

The Revolution uses a 1-inch chrome steel rod with a precision linear motion bearing. No track. No rollers. Nothing to jump, nothing to fill with grit, nothing to wear and sag. The panel rides the bearing — period.

  • 1" chrome steel rod + linear motion bearing drive
  • Structural I-beam header — no deflection under load
  • DC motor, 1/5 HP with Kevlar timing belt
  • Microprocessor controller — self-diagnoses, displays fault codes
  • Full perimeter breakout capability (FBO configurations)
  • Magnetic breakaway sidelights — no ball catches
  • Full-height extruded interlocks — no air gap
  • Electronics manufactured in the USA

Configurations

The Revolution ships in three primary breakout configurations — specify by opening type.

🔄

ESA — Electro-Swing Away

Standard configuration. Panels retract into the pocket. Sidelights are fixed. Ideal for most commercial entrances where breakout is not required by code.

🚪

FBO — Full Breakout Open

Both active panels and sidelights break out in the direction of egress. Required by many AHJs for emergency egress in high-occupancy applications. Download FBO drawings →

↔️

FSL — Full Side Light

Full-height sidelight panels that breakout independently. Used in vestibule configurations and where the sidelight width contributes to the required egress width.

Bi-parting or single slide? Both are available in all three breakout configurations. Specify LH or RH swing for single-slide FBO/FSL. Standard bi-parting opening is SO-SX-SX-SO. Custom panel splits available on request.
